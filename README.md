Outpatient Monitoring System (OPD)
Overview
The Outpatient Monitoring System (OPD) is designed to enhance healthcare management by allowing hospital staff to track and support patients requiring follow-up care after hospital visits. The system helps manage patient records, schedule appointments, and distinguish between treated and untreated patients, promoting timely follow-ups and continuous care.

Key Features
Login and Sign Up: Secure login and user registration to ensure authorized access.
Patient Management: View, add, search, and manage patient details, including their medical history and personal information.
Treatment Tracking: Categorize patients based on treatment status (treated or untreated) and provide an overview of patient records.
Medical Records Entry: Add and update patient medical records, including treatment details, diagnoses, and vital statistics.
Appointment Scheduling: Update and manage patient appointments seamlessly.
Data Export: Export patient lists for record-keeping and reporting purposes.
User Dashboard: Provides an overview of patient statistics and condition-specific data.
User Roles
Admin: The primary user responsible for managing patient data, adding and updating patient information, and overseeing system functions.
Functional Requirements
Login: User authentication to access the system.
Sign Up: Register new admin users with username, email, and password.
Patient List: View and manage patient records.
Add New Patient: Enter personal and contact details to add new patients.
Search Patient: Find patient records using name or ID.
Categorize Patients: Identify and list treated and untreated patients for follow-ups.
Add Medical Record: Enter new treatment records and update patient history.
Update Appointment: Change or update scheduled patient appointments.
Logout: Securely log out from the system.
Non-functional Requirements
Security: Data encryption to protect patient information.
Usability: User-friendly interfaces for easy navigation.
Performance: Fast response time and multi-user support.
Maintainability: Modular codebase for future updates and fixes.
Data Consistency: Real-time updates to patient data.
User Interface Details
Login/Sign-Up Screens: Forms for user authentication and registration.
Dashboard: Displays patient statistics, treatment status, and quick navigation tools.
Patient Details Page: Shows comprehensive patient data, including personal information and treatment records.
Medical Record Entry: Interface to input new medical details and view treatment history.
Appointment Management: Tools for scheduling and updating appointments.

Usage Guide
Logging In: Enter your registered email and password on the login screen.
Adding a New Patient: Click the "Add Patient" button on the dashboard and fill in the form.
Viewing Patient Details: Click the "Details" button to see full patient information.
Updating Appointments: Select a patient and choose the "Update Appointment" option.
Logging Out: Click the "Log Out" button in the sidebar.
